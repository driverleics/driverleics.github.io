ABOUT DRIVERLEICS - AUTONOMOUS DRIVING

Studies indicate an increasing demand for autonomous vehicles and there is no secret why. Having identified the ‘elimination of accidents due to manual errors; reduced carbon dioxide emissions due to lower consumption of fuel and high penetration capabilities into the automotive sector’ as a few benefits of autonomous vehicles. It is quite evident why there is an increasing interest in the field - we may all be sitting as passengers in one of these self-driving cars in the not too distant future. Therefore, ensuring their quality (e.g. safety and trustworthiness) is paramount.
Our research concerns the quality of the software for autonomous vehicles from the model-based development and automated testing perspectives. The DriverLeics research group develops various algorithms for autonomous vehicles in complex urban scenarios. These algorithms are designed to test the full capabilities of a simulated autonomous model against an autonomous agent. The challenges include accurate self-localisation, detection and classification of pedestrians against cyclists/vehicles and the safe completion of driving routes.
